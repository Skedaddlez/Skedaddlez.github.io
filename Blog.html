Website Overhaul and Portable Wii

Website Overhaul and Portable Wii
28/07/2020
Been a hot minute since I've worked on this website but I thought it could use a touch-up and a new blog post.

I have updated alot of my goals and similar to better reflect my desire to work in health to some capacity. When I originally made this website, I was quite burnt out on health and clinical science, but I have since come around to the idea of working in that industry again. I am not regretting my change to electrical engineering, as it allows me to learn alot about a hobby of mine, but I hope to work as a biomedical engineer one day, making life-changing machines!

I also added my portable Wii to my project list. I have been talking about doing this for years and I finally started about a month and a half ago. I'm a decent portion of my way through the project at the moment, having just completed a successful trim of the Wii. Next step is a whole bunch of 3D printing, as well as some casework for the shell.

After the Wii, I want to work on some smaller stuff, probably dip back into Arduino or Raspberry Pi. Alternatively, I might begin working on some custom electronics stuff using KiCad. Who knows? That could be a long way off yet...

The Prusa cometh...

The Prusa cometh...
Over the weekend, my Prusa i3 MK3S arrived! After hours and hours and hours of building followed by more hours of calibration, I finally am getting some decent prints. So far, I am very impressed by the ability of this printer, even despite the price tag. My plan is to use this to make enclosures to my projects, as well as repairing and embellishing other things when it makes sense. One particular print I am looking forward to is the case for my Wii portable I plan to make this year!

23/04/2019

One likely dead Uno later...

One likely dead Uno later...
In trying to get get a second GC randomiser circuit up and running, I managed to overheat an Arduino Uno clone's ATmega328 chip to a temperature easily high enough to burn my fingers off.

After hours slaving away, turns out I had been looking at the wrong pinout the whole time - I needed the female and I was looking at the male. I was surging the poor Arduino with a negative voltage as a result, as 5V and ground were mirrored from male to female. Whoops.

It also cost me at least an extra GC extension cable, but hey, you learn.


- Stephen
